The Place

For One Night,
Step Out Of The Ordinary

The Algonquin was created for people who still believe an evening can be more than a table, a drink, and a receipt.

Inside, you'll find an intimate private club inspired by the spirit of the Algonquin Round Table of 1920s New York — a place where writers, thinkers, artists, and interesting people gathered not to perform, but to converse.

  • No cameras.
  • No scrolling.
  • No broadcasting.
  • No rush.

Just a room, a drink, a meal, and the sort of conversation that becomes difficult to find in public places.

The House Rules

The Rules
Of The House

The Algonquin is members club, not a normal bar or restaurant.
That is the point.

To protect the atmosphere of the club, all guests agree to the following:

No phones. No photos. No recording.

The Algonquin is an analog space. The evening belongs to the people in the room.

Dress properly.

No flip-flops. No t-shirts. Long pants are preferred.

Reserve before arriving.

Guest passes are issued by reservation only.

Respect the room.

The club is private, intimate, and deliberately not for everyone.
